About ¶

I’m a programmer with an interest in functional languages, systems programming, and retrocomputing. I’ve been writing Lisp on and off for over two decades and, nowadays, contributing to the Bitcoin ecosystem.

In a previous phase of my professional career, I worked as a CTO, technical director, software architect and engineer at large enterprises. That was a valuable period, and the experience I accumulated there is now applied to projects that benefit from a technical and organizational perspective, without necessarily being tied to that same context.

This blog is where I work through ideas in public. Posts tend to be technical, but I try to write them so they’re readable without a running REPL :-D.
